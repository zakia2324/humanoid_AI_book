# Glossary

This glossary provides definitions for key terms used throughout the textbook on Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics.

---

**Actuator (حرکت دینے والا - Harkat Dainay Wala):** A component responsible for moving or controlling a mechanism or system. Often electric motors or hydraulic cylinders in robots.

**Adaptive Control:** A control system that can automatically modify its parameters in response to changes in the robot's dynamics or the environment.

**Agent (کارندہ - Karinda):** In Reinforcement Learning, the learner and decision-maker (e.g., the robot).

**Affordances:** Design elements that clearly suggest how an object or interface can be used. In HRI, robot behaviors that suggest interaction.

**AI Consciousness:** The philosophical and scientific debate about whether Artificial Intelligence can achieve true consciousness or self-awareness.

**Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR):** The technology that converts spoken language into text.

**Bipedal Locomotion (دو پایا حرکت - Do Paya Harkat):** The ability of a robot to move on two legs.

**Closed-Loop Control:** A control system that uses sensor feedback to adjust its actions and correct deviations from a desired state.

**Cognitive Robotics (ادراکی روبوٹکس - Idraaki Robotics):** A field aiming to build robots that can reason, learn, adapt, and interact with the world in a way that resembles human cognition.

**Compliance (لچک - Lachak):** The ability of a robot to yield or deform when external forces are applied, rather than rigidly resisting them. Crucial for safe HRI.

**Contact-Rich Tasks:** Robotic tasks that involve continuous physical contact with the environment or objects.

**Correspondence Problem:** In imitation learning, the challenge of mapping observed human movements to a robot's own joint configurations and kinematics.

**Cybernetics:** The interdisciplinary study of control and communication in living organisms, machines, and organizations.

**Decentralized Control:** A control architecture where decision-making is distributed among multiple agents or components, rather than residing in a single central unit.

**Deep Learning (DL):** A subset of Machine Learning using artificial neural networks with multiple layers to learn complex patterns from data.

**Degrees of Freedom (آزادی کی ڈگریاں - Azadi Ki Digriyaan):** The number of independent parameters that define the configuration or state of a mechanical system.

**Depth Cameras:** Vision sensors (e.g., LiDAR, Structured Light, Time-of-Flight) that directly measure the distance to objects, providing 3D spatial information.

**Dynamics:** The study of the relationship between forces, torques, and the resulting motion of a robot, considering mass, inertia, and external forces.

**End-Effector (آخری عامل - Aakhri Aamil / پکڑنے والا - Pakarnay Wala):** The device at the end of a robotic arm, designed to interact with the environment (e.g., gripper, tool).

**Environment (ماحول - Mahol):** In Reinforcement Learning, the physical world or context the agent interacts with.

**Ethical Guidelines:** Principles established to guide the responsible development and deployment of AI and robotics, emphasizing aspects like transparency, accountability, and fairness.

**Exteroceptive Sensors:** Sensors that gather information about the robot's external environment (e.g., cameras, LiDAR).

**Force Closure Grasp:** A grasping strategy where the gripper applies sufficient forces to an object to prevent it from moving, relying on friction.

**Force-Torque Sensors:** Devices that measure the forces and torques applied to a robot's joints or end-effector.

**Forward Kinematics:** The calculation of the end-effector's position and orientation given the joint angles of a robot.

**Generalization (عمومیت - Umumiyat):** The ability of a learned model or policy to perform well on new data or in new environments not seen during training.

**Grasping Strategies (مضبوط پکڑ - Mazboot Pakar):** The methods and techniques used by robots to pick up and hold objects securely.

**Hybrid Control:** A robot control architecture that combines elements of both hierarchical planning and reactive execution.

**Human-Centered Design:** A design philosophy that prioritizes the needs, expectations, and capabilities of human users.

**Human-Robot Interaction (HRI):** The study of how humans and robots can communicate and collaborate effectively, safely, and intuitively.

**Humanoid Robotics (انسانی روبوٹ - Insani Robot):** A specialized branch of robotics focused on building robots that resemble the human body in form and often in behavior.

**Imitation Learning (نقالی کے ذریعے سیکھنا - Naqqali ke Zariye Seekhna):** A learning paradigm where a robot learns a policy by observing expert demonstrations.

**Impedance Control:** A control strategy that regulates a robot's dynamic interaction with its environment, defining how it resists or yields to external forces.

**Inertial Measurement Units (IMUs):** Sensors (accelerometers and gyroscopes) providing data on orientation, angular velocity, and linear acceleration, crucial for balance.

**Inverse Kinematics:** The calculation of the joint angles required to achieve a desired end-effector position and orientation.

**Kinematics:** The study of the motion of robots without considering the forces that cause the motion; describes position, velocity, and acceleration.

**Learning from Demonstration (LfD) (مظاہرے سے سیکھنا - Muzahirey se Seekhna):** A broad approach where a robot extracts skills or policies from human demonstrations.

**Liability (ذمہ داری - Zimmedari):** Legal responsibility for an action or event; a key ethical consideration in robotics for determining fault in case of accidents.

**LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging):** A remote sensing method that uses pulsed laser light to measure distances, creating 3D representations of environments.

**Localization (مقام کا تعین - Maqam ka Tayyun):** The process of determining a robot's position and orientation within a map.

**Machine Learning (ML):** A type of AI that enables systems to learn from data without explicit programming.

**Mapping (نقشہ سازی - Naqsha Saazi):** The process of creating a representation of an environment by a robot.

**Metacognition:** The ability to reflect on one's own knowledge and learning processes; a concept in cognitive robotics.

**Monocular Cameras:** Standard 2D cameras used for object recognition, color detection, and tracking.

**Motor Primitives:** Basic, reusable building blocks of movement that can be combined to generate complex robotic behaviors.

**Multi-Finger Hands:** Robotic grippers that mimic human hands with multiple actuated or underactuated fingers for enhanced dexterity.

**Multi-Robot Systems (متعدد روبوٹ سسٹم - Mutaddid Robot System):** Multiple sophisticated robots working collaboratively on a task.

**Multi-modal Perception:** Combining information from various sensor types (e.g., vision, touch, hearing) for a more complete understanding of the environment.

**Object Recognition:** The ability of a robot to identify and classify objects within its environment, typically using computer vision.

**Offline Learning (غیر حاضرانہ سیکھنا - Ghair Hazirana Seekhna):** Robot learning that occurs in simulation or with pre-collected datasets before deployment to the physical world.

**Online Learning (حاضرانہ سیکھنا - Hazirana Seekhna):** Robot learning that occurs directly from interactions with the real world during operation.

**Open-Loop Control:** A control system where the robot executes pre-programmed commands without feedback from sensors.

**Path Planning (راستہ کی منصوبہ بندی - Rasta ki Mansuba Bandi):** The process of generating a collision-free path for a robot from a start to a goal location.

**Physical AI:** Artificial Intelligence systems embodied in physical agents (like robots) that interact with the real world.

**Policy (پالیسی - Policy):** In Reinforcement Learning, the agent's strategy for choosing actions based on its current state.

**Proprioceptive Sensors:** Sensors that measure the robot's internal state (e.g., joint position, force on a joint).

**Reinforcement Learning (RL):** A Machine Learning paradigm where an agent learns to make decisions through trial and error by maximizing a reward signal from its environment.

**Reward (انعام - Inaam):** In Reinforcement Learning, a feedback signal indicating the desirability of an action.

**Robot (روبوٹ - Robot):** An automated machine designed to perform tasks, especially one programmable by computer.

**Scene Understanding:** The ability of a robot to interpret the context, relationships, and typical arrangement of multiple objects within an environment.

**Sensor (محسوس کرنے والا آلہ - Mehsoos Karnay Wala Aala / حسیات - Hissiyaat):** A device that detects and responds to input from the physical environment.

**Sensor Fusion (حسیاتی ادغام - Hissiyaati Idgham):** The process of combining data from multiple sensors to improve the accuracy and reliability of perception.

**Skill Acquisition:** The process by which a robot develops new capabilities or improves existing ones through practice, learning, or instruction.

**SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping):** A computational problem of constructing or updating a map of an unknown environment while simultaneously keeping track of an agent's location within it.

**Soft Grippers:** Grippers made of compliant materials, ideal for handling delicate or irregularly shaped objects.

**Soft Robotics (نرم روبوٹکس - Naram Robotics):** A subfield of robotics that focuses on designing robots using compliant, deformable materials.

**State (حالت - Halat):** In Reinforcement Learning, the current situation of the agent and environment.

**Stereo Cameras:** Pairs of cameras that mimic human binocular vision, used for depth perception and 3D reconstruction.

**Stiffness Control:** Regulating the apparent stiffness of a robot's joints to achieve desired interaction properties.

**Strong AI:** The hypothesis that a machine can truly possess a mind and cognitive abilities akin to a human.

**Supervised Learning:** A type of Machine Learning that learns from labeled data (input-output pairs).

**Swarm Robotics (جھنڈ روبوٹکس - Jhund Robotics):** The study of large numbers of relatively simple robots exhibiting complex collective behaviors through local interactions.

**Symbolic AI (Rule-Based AI):** An AI paradigm focusing on explicit knowledge representation and logical reasoning based on predefined rules.

**Tactile Sensors:** Pressure-sensitive elements that detect contact, pressure distribution, and texture.

**Theory of Mind:** The ability to attribute mental states (beliefs, desires, intentions) to oneself and others; a goal in cognitive robotics.

**Transfer Learning:** Applying knowledge gained from solving one problem to a different but related problem.

**Underactuated Hands:** Robot hands with fewer actuators than joints, relying on mechanical design for compliance and grasping.

**Unsupervised Learning:** A type of Machine Learning that finds patterns in unlabeled data.

**Weak AI:** The hypothesis that machines can only simulate thinking and do not truly possess a mind or consciousness.

**Zero Moment Point (ZMP):** A point on the ground where the total moment of all forces acting on a bipedal robot is zero, crucial for maintaining balance.
