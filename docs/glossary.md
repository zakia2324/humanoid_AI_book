# Glossary

![Robotics Glossary](https://source.unsplash.com/random/800x450/?dictionary,robotics_terms)

This glossary provides definitions for key terms used throughout "Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics."

*   **Actuator:** A component of a robot that converts energy into motion, typically used to control joints or manipulate objects.
*   **Aitbaar (اعتبار):** Urdu term for "trust" or "credibility," emphasizing its importance in human-robot coexistence.
*   **Anthropomorphism:** The attribution of human characteristics, emotions, or behavior to animals, inanimate objects, or robots.
*   **Autonomous Mobile Robot (AMR):** A robot that can understand and navigate its environment independently, without being confined to fixed paths.
*   **Bipedal Locomotion:** The ability to move using two legs, a defining characteristic of humanoid robots.
*   **Cognitive Architecture:** A theoretical framework or computational model that aims to integrate perception, reasoning, learning, and action into a coherent intelligent system.
*   **Collaborative Robot (Cobot):** A robot designed to work alongside humans in a shared workspace, assisting with tasks.
*   **Compliance:** The ability of a robot joint or limb to yield or deform in response to external forces, often for safety or adaptability.
*   **Computer Vision:** A field of artificial intelligence that enables computers and systems to derive meaningful information from digital images, videos, and other visual inputs.
*   **Convolutional Neural Network (CNN):** A class of deep neural networks, primarily used for analyzing visual imagery.
*   **Dana-O-Bina (دانا و بینا):** Urdu phrase meaning "wise and discerning," used to describe the integrated intelligence of cognitive robots.
*   **Deep Learning (DL):** A subfield of machine learning that uses artificial neural networks with multiple layers (deep neural networks) to learn complex patterns from data.
*   **Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL):** The combination of deep neural networks with reinforcement learning, enabling agents to learn complex behaviors from high-dimensional sensor data.
*   **Degrees of Freedom (DoF):** The number of independent parameters that define the configuration or state of a mechanical system, such as a robot arm.
*   **Dexterous Manipulation:** The ability to perform complex, fine motor tasks with precision and coordination, often involving in-hand object re-positioning.
*   **Domain Randomization:** A technique used in sim-to-real transfer where various parameters in a simulation are randomized during training to make a learned policy more robust to real-world variations.
*   **Dynamics:** The study of motion and its causes (forces and torques), considering mass, inertia, and external influences on a robot.
*   **Embodied AI:** Intelligent systems integrated with physical bodies, enabling them to interact directly with the real world through perception and action.
*   **Embodied Cognition Hypothesis:** The theory that intelligence emerges from the interaction of a mind with a body and its environment.
*   **Force Closure:** A type of grasp where forces applied by the gripper prevent any movement of the object, assuming sufficient friction.
*   **Force/Torque Sensors:** Sensors that measure the forces and torques exerted by or on a robot, crucial for compliant motion and interaction.
*   **Form Closure:** A type of grasp where the geometry of the gripper completely encloses the object, preventing motion regardless of friction.
*   **Forward Kinematics:** The calculation of a robot's end-effector position and orientation given its joint angles.
*   **Hakeem (حکیم):** Urdu term for a skilled physician or wise person, used to describe the precision and dedication of healthcare robots.
*   **Harkat (حرکت):** Urdu term for "motion" or "movement," used in analogy for kinematics.
*   **Hifazat (حفاظت):** Urdu term for "safety" or "protection."
*   **Hooshmand Raah-numa (ہوشمند رہنما):** Urdu phrase meaning "wise guide/strategist," used to describe goal-directed planning in cognitive robots.
*   **Human-Robot Interaction (HRI):** The study of interactions between humans and robots, focusing on design, communication, and collaboration.
*   **Hunarmand (ہنرمند):** Urdu term for a "skilled craftsman," used to describe the embodied experience of Physical AI.
*   **Ilm (علم):** Urdu term for "knowledge" or "science."
*   **Imdadi Haath (امدادی ہاتھ):** Urdu phrase meaning "helping hand."
*   **Imitation Learning:** A machine learning paradigm where an agent learns a policy by observing demonstrations of desired behavior.
*   **Impedance Control:** A control strategy that regulates the relationship between applied force and resulting motion, making a robot behave like a spring-damper system.
*   **Inverse Kinematics:** The calculation of a robot's joint angles required to achieve a desired end-effector position and orientation.
*   **Jaan (جان):** Urdu term for "life" or "soul," used in analogy for a robot's power system.
*   **Jaan-Nisar (جان نثار):** Urdu phrase meaning "adventurer ready to sacrifice," used for exploration robots.
*   **Jasoos (جاسوس):** Urdu term for "detective," used in analogy for sensor fusion.
*   **Kinematics:** The study of the geometry of motion without considering the forces involved.
*   **Lidar (Light Detection and Ranging):** A remote sensing method that uses pulsed laser light to measure distances and create 3D maps.
*   **Machine Learning (ML):** A field of artificial intelligence that enables systems to learn from data without being explicitly programmed.
*   **Manzil (منزل):** Urdu term for "destination."
*   **Model-Based Reinforcement Learning:** An RL approach where the agent explicitly learns or uses a model of the environment's dynamics.
*   **Model-Free Reinforcement Learning:** An RL approach where the agent learns directly from interactions without building an explicit model of the environment.
*   **Momentum Control:** A balance control strategy for humanoids that actively uses the robot's angular and linear momentum to absorb impacts and maintain stability.
*   **Multi-Contact Locomotion:** Robot movement strategy that utilizes multiple contact points (e.g., feet, hands, knees) with the environment for enhanced stability and mobility.
*   **Naqqal (نقّال):** Urdu term for an "imitator" or "mimic," used to describe biologically inspired design.
*   **Nakkaash (نقاش):** Urdu term for an "artisan" or "engraver," used to describe dexterous manipulation.
*   **Pehlwan (پہلوان):** Urdu term for a "wrestler," used in analogy for full-body manipulation.
*   **Pehraydar (پہرے دار):** Urdu term for a "guard" or "watchman."
*   **Physical AI:** Artificial intelligence systems integrated with physical bodies, enabling direct interaction with the real world.
*   **Policy (π):** In Reinforcement Learning, a mapping from states to actions that defines an agent's behavior.
*   **Proprioceptive Sensors:** Sensors that provide information about the internal state of a robot, such as joint angles, motor speeds, and accelerations.
*   **Quwwat (قوت):** Urdu term for "power" or "strength," used in analogy for dynamics.
*   **Reality Gap:** The discrepancy between a simulated environment and the real world, making direct transfer of learned policies challenging.
*   **Reinforcement Learning (RL):** A machine learning paradigm where an agent learns to make decisions by performing actions in an environment to maximize a cumulative reward.
*   **Robot:** A machine capable of carrying out a complex series of actions automatically, especially one programmable by computer.
*   **Robo-ethics:** The ethical considerations and guidelines specifically related to the design, development, and use of robots.
*   **Sab Ek Saath, Ek Maqsad Ke Liye (سب ایک ساتھ، ایک مقصد کے لیے):** Urdu phrase meaning "all together for one purpose," used for whole-body control.
*   **Sarparast (سرپرست):** Urdu term for a "guardian" or "patron."
*   **Sarparast (سرپرست):** Urdu term for a "guardian" or "patron."
*   **Sensor Fusion:** The process of combining data from multiple sensors to obtain a more accurate and comprehensive understanding of the environment.
*   **Series Elastic Actuator (SEA):** An actuator that includes a spring in series with the motor, providing compliance and improving force control and energy efficiency.
*   **Shagird (شاگرد):** Urdu term for an "apprentice," used in analogy for imitation learning.
*   **Sim-to-Real Transfer:** The process of training a robot in a simulated environment and then transferring the learned policy to a physical robot.
*   **Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM):** The computational problem of building a map of an unknown environment while simultaneously tracking the robot's location within that map.
*   **Soft Robotics:** A subfield of robotics that deals with the design, control, and fabrication of robots from highly compliant materials, often inspired by biological organisms.
*   **Taaqat (طاقت):** Urdu term for "power" or "strength."
*   **Task Prioritization:** A concept in whole-body control where multiple tasks are assigned priorities, with lower-priority tasks executed in the null space of higher-priority ones.
*   **Transformers:** A deep learning architecture that has revolutionized natural language processing and is increasingly used in computer vision for tasks requiring long-range dependencies.
*   **Trust:** A firm belief in the reliability, truth, ability, or strength of someone or something; critical for human-robot interaction.
*   **Ustad (استاد):** Urdu term for a "master" or "teacher," used in analogy for imitation learning.
*   **Variable Stiffness Actuation (VSA):** The ability to actively change the stiffness of a robot joint or limb, offering benefits in safety, efficiency, and dexterity.
*   **Whole-Body Control (WBC):** A control approach for humanoids that coordinates all joints and limbs simultaneously to achieve complex tasks while maintaining balance and respecting constraints.
*   **Zero Moment Point (ZMP):** A key concept in bipedal locomotion, representing the point on the ground about which the sum of all moments of active forces and gravity equals zero, used to maintain dynamic stability.
